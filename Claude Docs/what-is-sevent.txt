 SEVENT — Your Gateway to the Events World
 What the Platform Is, Who It Serves, and How It Works
==============================================================
 A plain-language overview for colleagues and management.
 No technical detail — just what Sevent is and why it matters.
 Prepared: 2026-05-14
==============================================================


--------------------------------------------------------------
 1. IN ONE PARAGRAPH
--------------------------------------------------------------

Sevent is a Saudi digital marketplace for the events
industry. It connects the people who organize events with the
suppliers who service them — sound and lighting, catering,
photography, staging, equipment rental, and more — in one
trusted, transparent place. Instead of chasing vendors through
WhatsApp, personal contacts, and word of mouth, an organizer
posts what they need once, receives competing quotes from
verified suppliers, compares them side by side, and books — all
on a single platform with a clear record of every step.


--------------------------------------------------------------
 2. THE PROBLEM SEVENT SOLVES
--------------------------------------------------------------

The Saudi events sector is large and growing fast, but the way
buyers and suppliers find each other is still fragmented and
informal. Today:

  - HARD TO FIND THE RIGHT SUPPLIER. There is no single trusted
    reference that lists vendors with verified credentials,
    real ratings, and clear scope.

  - NO PRICE TRANSPARENCY. Prices depend on who you know.
    The same service can cost very different amounts depending
    on the relationship, which opens the door to favoritism
    and inconsistency.

  - WASTED TIME. Organizers spend weeks sourcing vendors for a
    single event — time that should go into planning and
    creativity, not phone tag.

  - QUALITY IS A GAMBLE. With no reliable review system, bad
    experiences repeat themselves and suppliers are never truly
    held accountable.

Sevent replaces that scattered, relationship-driven process
with a structured, accountable one.


--------------------------------------------------------------
 3. THE MARKET CONTEXT
--------------------------------------------------------------

Saudi Arabia's events and entertainment sector is expanding
rapidly under Vision 2030 — through seasons and festivals,
business and cultural events, tourism growth, and major
upcoming international events. This creates two pressures at
once:

  - More events every year means more demand for organizers
    to source suppliers quickly and reliably.
  - A national push toward formal, digital business means the
    informal, offline way of doing events is increasingly out
    of step with how the rest of the economy works.

Sevent is positioned in that gap: a local, Arabic-first
platform built specifically for how the Saudi events market
actually operates.

(Note: this section is market context, not a financial
projection. Sizing and forecasts are out of scope for this
document.)


--------------------------------------------------------------
 4. WHO SEVENT SERVES
--------------------------------------------------------------

Sevent is a TWO-SIDED platform. Today it focuses on the
business-to-business side; serving individual consumers
directly is a planned future expansion.

  A) EVENT ORGANIZERS (the buyers) — TODAY
     Professional event planners, companies, and teams that
     run events and need to source services for them. They
     want one trusted reference instead of a hundred separate
     conversations, clear pricing, and a single place to track
     all their events.

  B) SERVICE SUPPLIERS / VENDORS (the sellers) — TODAY
     Event-service businesses and freelancers — equipment
     rental, catering, photography, staging, and the rest.
     Many are small or medium businesses with strong work but
     weak digital visibility. They want a professional profile,
     a steady stream of qualified requests, and an easier way
     to manage their bookings and calendar.

  C) INDIVIDUAL ORGANIZERS (the public) — PLANNED
     Individuals planning personal events such as weddings and
     family occasions. This consumer-facing experience is a
     planned next stage, not part of the current platform.

  D) THE SEVENT TEAM (administrators)
     An internal admin layer reviews and verifies suppliers,
     monitors activity, handles feedback, and keeps the
     marketplace trustworthy.

 6. HOW THE PLATFORM WORKS — THE CORE JOURNEY
--------------------------------------------------------------

The heart of Sevent is a clear, repeatable journey from
"I need something" to "it's booked and reviewed."

  STEP 1 — CREATE THE EVENT
  The organizer sets up their event (type, city, venue,
  dates, budget) as the home for everything related to it.

  STEP 2 — REQUEST QUOTES (THE RFQ)
  For each service the event needs, the organizer creates a
  Request for Quote describing exactly what they want. They
  can invite specific suppliers, let the platform suggest
  good matches, or both.

  STEP 3 — SUPPLIERS RESPOND
  Invited suppliers submit quotes. Suppliers can also
  discover open requests themselves through a marketplace of
  opportunities and apply directly. Where needed, an
  organizer can ask a supplier for a fuller technical
  proposal.

  STEP 4 — COMPARE AND CHOOSE
  The organizer sees all quotes for a request in one
  comparison view — price, scope, and supplier rating
  together — and can export or print it for internal review.

  STEP 5 — BOOK AND CONFIRM
  The organizer accepts a quote, which creates a booking.
  The supplier confirms (or declines), and the booking
  becomes the shared record of the agreement.

  STEP 6 — DELIVER AND REVIEW
  After the event, organizer and supplier review each other.
  Reviews are tied to real bookings, so a supplier's
  reputation on Sevent is earned, not claimed.

Throughout the journey, both sides get in-app notifications
and email updates, and can message each other and the Sevent
support team.


--------------------------------------------------------------
 7. WHAT SUPPLIERS GET
--------------------------------------------------------------

  - GUIDED ONBOARDING. A step-by-step setup for business
    details, service categories, and documents — with a
    path for both freelancers and registered companies.

  - VERIFICATION BADGE. Suppliers submit official documents
    (commercial registration, VAT, banking, address, company
    profile). Once the Sevent team approves them, they are
    marked as verified — which builds buyer trust.

  - A PROFESSIONAL PUBLIC PROFILE. A complete, customizable
    page showing services, packages, pricing, a photo and
    video portfolio, location, and reviews.

  - A SERVICE CATALOG. Suppliers build packages and pricing
    rules so quoting is fast and consistent.

  - QUALIFIED REQUESTS. Instead of cold leads, suppliers
    receive structured requests that match their category
    and area — plus a marketplace of open opportunities they
    can pursue.

  - A QUOTE BUILDER. Suppliers respond with clear, itemized
    quotes; every version sent is kept as a record.

  - BOOKINGS AND CALENDAR. An availability calendar and
    booking management, so suppliers can track what is held,
    confirmed, or open.

  - A DASHBOARD THAT GROWS WITH THEM. The supplier's home
    screen adapts to where they are — onboarding, pending
    review, or fully approved and active.


--------------------------------------------------------------
 8. WHAT ORGANIZERS GET
--------------------------------------------------------------

  - DISCOVERY AND SEARCH. Browse suppliers by service
    category and explore verified, published profiles.

  - ONE REQUEST, MANY SUPPLIERS. Send a single structured
    request and reach multiple verified suppliers at once.

  - SIDE-BY-SIDE COMPARISON. Evaluate all quotes for a
    request together, with export and print options for
    internal sign-off.

  - EVENT MANAGEMENT IN ONE PLACE. Track every event, every
    request, and every booking from one dashboard.

  - VERIFIED, ACCOUNTABLE SUPPLIERS. Every bookable supplier
    has been checked, and reviews come only from real
    bookings.

  - A RECORD OF EVERYTHING. The full request-to-booking trail
    is kept, so agreements and decisions are documented.

 9. SERVICE CATEGORIES

Sevent organizes the events market into a clear, browsable

range of what an event needs, such as sound and lighting,

This shared taxonomy is what makes search, matching, and

 10. TRUST AND ACCOUNTABILITY
--------------------------------------------------------------

Trust is the product. Sevent builds it through several
layers working together:

  - SUPPLIER VERIFICATION before anyone can be booked.
  - REVIEWS TIED TO REAL BOOKINGS — no fake or anonymous
    ratings.
  - A DOCUMENTED TRAIL from request to quote to booking.
  - ADMIN OVERSIGHT — a dedicated team reviewing suppliers,
    monitoring activity, and handling feedback and disputes.
  - ARABIC-FIRST, BILINGUAL EXPERIENCE — the platform works
    fully in Arabic and English, built for the local market.


--------------------------------------------------------------
 11. THE BUSINESS MODEL
--------------------------------------------------------------

Sevent's revenue model is built on two main streams:

  1) COMMISSION ON BOOKINGS
     Sevent earns a percentage on confirmed bookings made
     through the platform. This ties Sevent's revenue
     directly to the value it creates: it only earns when a
     real deal happens between an organizer and a supplier.

  2) SUPPLIER SUBSCRIPTIONS
     Suppliers can pay for subscription tiers that give them
     more visibility, higher limits, and added features —
     recurring revenue that grows as the supplier base grows.

Together these create a model with more than one income
source, where both the transaction side and the recurring
side reinforce each other: more suppliers attract more
organizers, more organizers generate more bookings, and more
bookings make subscriptions more valuable.

(Additional streams — such as featured listings or
enterprise arrangements — may be considered later but are
not part of the core model today.)


--------------------------------------------------------------
 12. GROWTH PATH
--------------------------------------------------------------

The natural growth path for Sevent follows the structure of
the market itself:

  - DEEPEN THE TWO-SIDED MARKETPLACE — keep growing the
    verified supplier base and the organizer base in the
    cities where activity is highest.

  - EXPAND GEOGRAPHICALLY across the Kingdom's main event
    centers.

  - OPEN UP THE CONSUMER SIDE (B2C) — bring individuals
    planning personal events onto the platform once the
    professional marketplace is solid.

The strength of a marketplace is that it compounds: every
new supplier makes the platform more useful to organizers,
and every new organizer makes it more attractive to
suppliers.
